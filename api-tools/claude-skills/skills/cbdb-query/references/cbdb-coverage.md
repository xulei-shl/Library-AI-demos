# CBDB Data Coverage Details

## Overview

China Biographical Database (CBDB) is jointly developed by Harvard University Fairbank Center for Chinese Studies, Peking University Center for Ancient Chinese History Studies, and Academia Sinica Institute of History and Philology. It is one of the world's most comprehensive and highly structured Chinese historical figures databases.

## Time Coverage

| Period | Coverage | Data Richness |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| Pre-Qin | Limited | Sparse |
| Han-Tang | Moderate | Growing |
| **Tang Dynasty** | Extensive | **Rich** |
| **Song Dynasty** | Extensive | **Richest** |
| Yuan Dynasty | Moderate | Moderate |
| **Ming Dynasty** | Extensive | **Rich** |
| **Qing Dynasty** | Extensive | **Rich** |
| Late Qing-Republic | Limited | Sparse |

**Primary Coverage**: 7th-19th century (Tang to Qing dynasties)

## Person Coverage

### Scale
- **Total Persons**: ~641,568 (as of August 2024)
- **Annual Growth**: Tens of thousands of new entries

### Person Types

| Category | Examples | Coverage |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **Officials** | Civil officials, military officers | Extensive |
| **Scholars** | Confucian scholars, philosophers | Extensive |
| **Literati** | Poets, writers, calligraphers | Extensive |
| **Examination Candidates** | Jinshi, juren degree holders | Extensive |
| **Military Personnel** | Generals, admirals | Moderate |
| **Religious Figures** | Buddhist monks, Daoist priests | Moderate |
| **Merchants** | Prominent businessmen | Limited |
| **Artisans** | Skilled craftsmen | Limited |
| **Women** | Notable women in history | Growing |

### Geographic Coverage
- All provinces of historical China
- Includes frontier regions
- Covers diaspora communities

## Content Dimensions

### 1. Basic Information
- **Name**: Primary name, courtesy names, art names, aliases
- **Birth/Death**: Year, month, day when available
- **Gender**: Male/Female
- **Dynasty**: Primary period of activity

### 2. Spatial Information
- **Hometown**: Native place, ancestral home
- **Migration**: Movement history between locations
- **Official Locations**: Places where served as official
- **Death Place**: Location of death

### 3. Social Relationships
- **Family**: Father, mother, siblings, spouse, children
- **Teachers**: Academic mentors and instructors
- **Students**: Disciples and students taught
- **Friends**: Notable friendships
- **Colleagues**: Official colleagues
- **Patrons**: Political patrons and supporters
- **Associates**: Various social connections

### 4. Career Information
- **Official Positions**: All recorded positions
- **Entry Paths**: How entered government service
- **Examination Records**: Civil service examination results
- **Promotions/Demotions**: Career progression
- **Honorary Titles**: Prestigious titles received

### 5. Writings and Achievements
- **Literary Works**: Books, poems, essays
- **Calligraphy/Art**: Artistic works
- **Scholarly Contributions**: Academic writings
- **Notable Achievements**: Significant accomplishments

## Data Quality Notes

### Strengths
- Highly structured relational database
- Standardized name romanization
- Cross-referenced sources
- Regular updates and corrections

### Limitations
- Coverage biased toward elite males
- Some periods better documented than others
- Rural and lower-class figures underrepresented
- Women's biographies still growing

## When to Use CBDB

### Ideal Use Cases
- Researching Tang-Song-Ming-Qing officials
- Studying literati and scholars
- Tracing social networks
- Historical geography research
- Prosopographical studies
- Digital humanities projects

### May Not Be Suitable
- Pre-Qin figures (limited coverage)
- Republic period figures (limited coverage)
- Ordinary people without historical records
- Non-elite social groups

## Source References

CBDB data is compiled from:
- Standard dynastic histories
- Biographical dictionaries
- Local gazetteers
- Collected works of individuals
- Epitaphs and tomb inscriptions
- Examination records
- Official documents
